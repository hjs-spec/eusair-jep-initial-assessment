# Technical Proof: Immutability & Traceability ⚖️
### *Alignment with EU AI Act Article 12 (Record-keeping)*

This document outlines the cryptographic measures ensuring that HJS Protocol logs are tamper-proof and time-ordered.

## 1. Temporal Ordering (RFC 9562 / UUIDv7)
Every judgment receipt generated by the JEP Protocol uses **UUIDv7**.
- **Mechanism**: The first 48 bits represent a Unix timestamp in milliseconds.
- **Regulatory Value**: Ensures "time-ordered recording" as mandated by Art. 12, preventing backdating of audit trails.

## 2. Cryptographic Non-repudiation (Ed25519)
All tool-call authorizations are signed using the **Ed25519** signature scheme.
- **Integrity**: Any alteration to the payload (e.g., changing a 'Denied' to 'Approved') will invalidate the signature.
- **Authenticity**: Proves the judgment originated from the authorized HJS Foundation sidecar.

## 3. Distributed Verifiability
The proof-of-judgment can be verified by any third-party auditor (like EUSAiR) without access to the original AI model's internal weights, ensuring **Transparency (Art. 13)**.
