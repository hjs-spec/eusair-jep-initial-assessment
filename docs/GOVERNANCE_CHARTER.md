# HJS Foundation: Governance and Constitutional Principles 🏛️

The **HJS Foundation LTD.** is incorporated in Singapore as a **Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG)**. Our constitution is specifically customized to ensure the long-term neutrality and public availability of the JEP protocol.

## ⚖️ Core Statutory Safeguards

### 1. Non-Profit Mandate (Art. 7A & 7B)
- **Object**: Dedicated entirely to public service and promoting scientific development in AI accountability.
- **Profit Lock**: Strictly prohibits the distribution of dividends or bonuses to members. All revenue must be reinvested into protocol evolution.

### 2. Strategic Asset Lock-in (Art. 67A)
The Foundation acts as the **Legal Guardian** of core assets which are permanently locked:
- **Intellectual Property**: JEP Protocol Specifications and RDP standards.
- **Infrastructure**: Core domains (`humanjudgment.org`, `humanlayer.io`) and trademarks.
- **Licensing**: Core documents remain permanently open under **CC BY-SA 4.0**.

### 3. Independent Governance (Art. 35A & 44)
To prevent "Regulatory Capture" or commercial bias:
- **Independent Directors**: A mandatory minimum of **1/3 of the Board** must be independent directors (non-sponsors and non-commercial affiliates).
- **Conflict of Interest**: Transactions with commercial subsidiaries must be approved by a majority of **Independent Directors** and disclosed publicly.

### 4. Dissolution Protection (Part 15)
In the event of winding up, any residual property **cannot be returned to members**. It must be transferred to another charitable or public-natured organization with a similar mission (e.g., global AI ethics or digital governance bodies).

## 🛠️ Operational Objects for EUSAiR Pilot
As per **Art. 7A(5)**, a primary object of the Foundation is:
> *"Collaborate with regulatory authorities... academic institutions, and industry alliances to promote the global adoption of responsible expression infrastructure."*

This pilot represents the Foundation's commitment to aligning JEP with the **EU AI Act** as a neutral, third-party verification layer.
